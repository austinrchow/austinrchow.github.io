Portfolio
Demo2Tutor. Last summer, I worked at Stanford's AI Lab, under Emma Brunskill and created an application that taught beginners rudimentary piano. We wanted to create a tool that would allow users with absolutely no musical experience to learn a famous Bach piece in as short amount of time as possible. With temporally clustered videos, our program determines how the user is performing and provides recommendations for which segments the user should continue to. Click here for a demo.
Tree to Tree. Last year, I was part of the CS + Social Good Studio class, a 2 quarter long endeavor focused on building a scalable social good project. Our team realized that many students on campus want to do community service but have not outlet for doing so. We decided to combat this problem by creating an iOS application that allows users to sign us for community service events around campus. Click here for a demo.
FlappyAI For this project, my team and I used deep reinforcement learning and transfer learning in order to train a FlappyBird model to perform at superhuman levels. We modeled the problem as a Markov Decision Process and we wanted to see whether or not our model trained on FlappyBird would perform just as well on a different, but similar game!
